About Project BISmark

BISmark, the Broadband Internet Service benchmark, is a project led by Georgia Tech and the University of Napoli to develop an OpenWrt- & CeroWrt-based platform for performing measurements of ISP performance, as well as network traffic inside the home.

Current status

BISmark currently has two active deployments: one in Atlanta, GA, United States and one in Cape Town, South Africa. This release is in support of our third deployment which will be located in a number of cities in the United States and Europe.

BISmark currently performs measurements of participants' broadband Internet connections using an active measurement suite that measures latency, throughput, and other connection characteristics throughout the day.

Future goals

The current status of the BISmark project represents only a small part of our plans for the project. Our plans for the future of the BISmark project and platform include:

  • A large, international deployment to enable measurement of many different topology arrangements, service providers, access technologies, and market and regulatory environments.
  • A passive measurement suite to measure home network user behavior and application usage.
  • The development of Network dashboard, a tool to enable users to monitor their Internet service performance and usage, and compare their performance and behavior with other participants.
  • The inclusion of the Kermit home network management interface, a user interface that seeks to reconceptualize home network management tasks in a more user-friendly way than conventional home router interfaces.
