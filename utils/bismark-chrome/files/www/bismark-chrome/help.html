Getting help with BISmark

BISmark is an experimental research project and is offered without support or warranty. We cannot provide support for your home network, any computers or devices connected to the BISmark router or your home network, or your Internet service.

We do have a community of BISmark users and developers that may be able to help you if you run into problems with your BISmark router. See below for how best to obtain support from this community.

How to get help

You should always attempt to first solve problems by yourself. If you are having a problem, begin by working through the BISmark documentation. If that does not help, then try one or more of the following:

  • Do not contact Netgear. They cannot help you with the BISmark software.
  • Read the BISmark documentation.
  • Attempt to isolate the cause or nature of the problem. It is much easier to provide help with specific problems statements ("DNS resolution is failing after I change my firewall settings") than vague problem statements ("Websites sometimes don't load").
  • Ask for help on the bismark-discuss mailing list. Members of the BISmark team and user community are on this list and can often provide help and assistance.
  • If you believe the problem is a fault with the BISmark software or system, please provide feedback so we can file a bug.

No Warranty

As described in the participation agreement, the BISmark router is provided "as is" with no warranty of any kind. We may be able to provide help or support from time to time, but we are not obligated to provide help or support.
